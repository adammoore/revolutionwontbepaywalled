Subject: Question about Open Access Options for My Research Dear [SUPERVISOR NAME], I hope this email finds you well. I've been thinking about publication strategy for my [dissertation chapter / paper] on [TOPIC], and I wanted to get your advice on open access options. I've been reading about Diamond Open Access journals (free to read and free to publish), and I'm wondering if they might be a good fit for this work. I found a few that seem relevant: - [JOURNAL 1] - [Brief note on why it's relevant] - [JOURNAL 2] - [Brief note] - [JOURNAL 3] - [Brief note] I'm particularly interested in Diamond OA because: 1. My work would be immediately accessible to researchers who don't have institutional subscriptions 2. I wouldn't need to find funding for APCs 3. Some of these journals have quick turnaround times That said, I know that publication venue matters for career prospects, and I don't want to jeopardize my future by publishing somewhere that won't be respected. **Questions for you:** - Are you familiar with any of these journals? Do they have good reputations in our field? - Do you think publishing in Diamond OA venues would hurt my career prospects? - Are there other Diamond OA journals you'd recommend I look at? - Would it make sense to aim for a Diamond OA journal for this paper and save [PRESTIGIOUS JOURNAL] for my stronger papers? I'd really value your perspective on this. I'm also happy to discuss over coffee if that's easier than email. Thank you for your guidance! Best, [YOUR NAME] --- P.S. I found some interesting information about the movement toward Diamond OA at https://bit.ly/_TRWBP_ if you're interested.

Tips for Students and ECRs

  • Do your research first: Find 3-5 legitimate Diamond OA journals in your field before approaching your supervisor
  • Frame as seeking advice: You're asking for guidance, not announcing a decision
  • Acknowledge career concerns: Show you understand the importance of publication venue
  • Be flexible: If your supervisor is skeptical, don't push too hard. Build evidence over time.
  • Connect with peers: Other grad students/ECRs may be having similar conversations

Finding Diamond OA Journals

Use the Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ) at doaj.org and filter by "No APC" to find Diamond OA journals in your field.
