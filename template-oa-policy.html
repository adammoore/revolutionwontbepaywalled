DRAFT POLICY PROPOSAL **[INSTITUTION NAME] Open Access Policy** **Effective Date:** [DATE] **Policy Owner:** [OFFICE/POSITION] **Review Date:** [DATE] --- **1. Purpose** This policy affirms [INSTITUTION]'s commitment to the broadest possible access to research outputs produced by its community. It establishes expectations for open access to publications and data, aligning with our mission of knowledge creation and public service. **2. Scope** This policy applies to all research articles authored or co-authored by [FACULTY / ALL RESEARCHERS] during their employment or affiliation with [INSTITUTION], accepted for publication on or after [DATE]. **3. Policy Statement** [INSTITUTION] faculty and researchers will make their peer-reviewed research articles openly accessible by: a) Depositing the final accepted manuscript in the [INSTITUTION] Institutional Repository within [30/60/90] days of publication, and b) Granting [INSTITUTION] a non-exclusive, irrevocable license to distribute their scholarly articles for any non-commercial purpose. **4. Priority for Diamond Open Access** While all open access is valued, [INSTITUTION] particularly encourages publication in Diamond Open Access journals (free to read and free to publish) as the most equitable and sustainable model for scholarly communication. **5. Implementation** **For Faculty:** - Deposit manuscripts in the institutional repository - Use the addendum to publication agreements when necessary (template provided by library) - Note: This policy does not require payment of APCs **For Library:** - Provide repository infrastructure and support - Maintain current information on Diamond OA journals by discipline - Assist with copyright addenda and publisher negotiations - Monitor compliance and report annually **For Administration:** - Allocate resources for repository maintenance - Consider OA publication record in promotion and tenure (but not mandate) - Support library efforts to redirect subscription funds to open infrastructure **6. Exemptions** Exemptions may be granted for: - Published works where copyright or license agreements prevent open access (after good-faith effort to modify) - Popular writings or works for general audiences - Other scholarly articles for which open access cannot be secured Faculty may request exemptions through [PROCESS]. **7. Support Mechanisms** [INSTITUTION] will: - Provide guidance on Diamond OA journal selection - Offer copyright advisory services - Maintain partnerships with open infrastructure organizations - NOT establish a central APC fund (to avoid incentivizing commercial OA over free alternatives) **8. Assessment Reform** In alignment with DORA (Declaration on Research Assessment), [INSTITUTION] will: - Not use journal impact factors in promotion, tenure, or hiring decisions - Evaluate research on its own merits - Value diverse publication venues including Diamond OA journals - Recognize open access as a positive contribution to research impact **9. Review and Revision** This policy will be reviewed every [2/3] years and revised as necessary to reflect evolving best practices in scholarly communication. **10. Resources** - [INSTITUTION] Institutional Repository: [URL] - Diamond OA journals by discipline: [URL] - Copyright guidance: [URL] - Open access FAQ: [URL] --- **Approval Process:** - Faculty Senate: [DATE] - Academic Council: [DATE] - [PROVOST/PRESIDENT]: [DATE] **Contact:** [NAME], [TITLE] [EMAIL] --- **Implementation Timeline:** | Phase | Dates | Actions | |-------|-------|---------| | Consultation | [DATES] | Faculty town halls, departmental discussions | | Pilot | [DATES] | Voluntary participation, test workflows | | Soft launch | [DATES] | Policy in effect, strong encouragement, high support | | Full implementation | [DATES] | Policy fully enforced, compliance monitoring |

Institutions with OA Policies

  • Harvard University - One of the first comprehensive faculty OA policies (2008)
  • MIT - Strong mandate with robust repository infrastructure
  • University of California - System-wide policy covering all campuses
  • Many European institutions - Required by national funders like Wellcome Trust, ERC

Key Considerations

  • Balance mandate with flexibility (exemption processes)
  • Provide strong infrastructure support
  • Don't create APC funds that incentivize expensive Gold OA
  • Combine with DORA principles for maximum impact
