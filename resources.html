Aligned Initiatives

The movement to reclaim scholarly publishing is global and growing. Here are recent initiatives, campaigns, and organizational efforts aligned with our vision:

Recent Developments (October-November 2025)

Invest in Open Infrastructure - Fund for Network Adoption

November 17, 2025: IOI announced inaugural grantees LA Referencia (Latin America) and UbuntuNet Alliance (Africa) will receive up to $1.5 million over three years. Selected from over 100 applications across 22 countries, with backing from Wellcome Trust, Digital Science, and multiple foundations.

November 14, 2025: EBSCO Information Services joined as a new financial supporter, with IOI emphasizing that demand far exceeds available resources.

November 12, 2025: IOI presented at FORM 2025 on who bears the financial burden of implementing open access mandates.

IOI LinkedIn | IOI Website

cOAlition S Strategy 2026-2030

November 2025: cOAlition S released its new strategic plan, having grown from 12 to 28 funders including agencies from Europe, North America, Jordan, Zambia, South Africa, and Australia, reflecting global momentum towards full and immediate open access.

View Strategy (PDF)

International Open Access Week 2025

October 20-26, 2025: Theme "Community over Commercialization" highlighted the importance of centering scholarly communities rather than commercial interests in open access publishing.

Publisher Restrictions Undermining Open Access

October 29, 2025: Authors Alliance reported that both Wiley and Elsevier added website restrictions stating "All rights are reserved, including those for text and data mining, AI training, and similar technologies," even for articles under CC BY licenses, fundamentally undermining open licensing.

Read the article

Digital Science: "Will 2025 be a turning point for Open Access?"

October 2025: Digital Science released analysis examining whether 2025 would mark a significant shift in the open access landscape, following years of incremental change.

Read the report

Diamond OA Stagnation: Evidence from EPFL

November 23, 2025: Abdelghani Maddi (CNRS - Sorbonne University) analyzed OA trends at EPFL (2018-2023), revealing that Diamond OA remained stagnant at approximately 3% despite overall OA growth. While closed-access declined from 55% to 28% and Hybrid OA grew via Read&Publish agreements, Diamond OA‚Äîthe only model "free to read and free to publish"‚Äîshowed virtually no growth.

Global Pattern Confirmed: Comments from experts revealed similar patterns worldwide:

  • Global DOAJ data (Mikael Laakso): Diamond OA remained stagnant (2020-2024) while APC-based articles grew from ~1M to 1.4M annually
  • Ukraine data (Myroslava Hladchenko): Diamond OA stable at 28.5% (2020-2023)‚Äînotably higher than EPFL but still stagnant

Key Insight: Stagnation reflects structural issues beyond funding, including prestige-driven incentives perpetuating commercial publisher dominance. The discussion highlighted the need for capital investment in Diamond infrastructure and cultural shifts challenging journal brand prestige.

View LinkedIn discussion

IMPACT-REPO Project Launch

March 2025: LIBER, OpenAIRE, SPARC Europe, and COAR launched IMPACT-REPO, a joint initiative to promote the vital position of open access repositories in Europe's research landscape.

Global Diamond Open Access Initiatives

2nd Global Summit on Diamond Open Access (2024)

Held in Cape Town, South Africa (December 8-14, 2024), this summit focused on "Diamond open access: centering social justice in scholarly communication to advance research as a public good."

Key Outcomes: The Toluca-Cape Town Declaration affirmed that "scholarly knowledge is a public good" and emphasized social justice, equity, and inclusivity as drivers of Diamond OA.

Learn more

UNESCO Diamond Open Access Initiative

Launched in September 2024, UNESCO engaged researchers, policymakers, publishers, and funders to explore equitable, community-driven, paywall-free publishing models aligned with Open Science principles.

UNESCO Diamond OA

European Research Area Diamond Capacity Hub (ERA-DCH)

Created in 2024 to facilitate equitable Open Access scholarly publishing without fees for readers or authors. Coordinates Diamond Capacity Centers (DCCs) across Europe, streamlining tools, services, and practices.

DIAMAS Project

A ‚Ç¨3 million EU-funded initiative involving 23 organizations to map the landscape of Diamond Open Access publishing in Europe over 36 months.

Editorial Board Resignations & Collective Action

Journal of Human Evolution Mass Resignation (December 2024)

The entire editorial board resigned from this premier paleoanthropology journal, citing Elsevier's "actions fundamentally incompatible with the ethos of the journal."

Key Issues:

  • Eliminated dedicated copy editor position (2019)
  • Unauthorized AI implementation causing formatting errors (2023)
  • Excessive article processing charges ($3,990)
  • Persistent demands to reduce associate editors

Policy & Reform Movements

Plan S / cOAlition S

2024 Annual Review: Noted slowing open access uptake and announced ending of financial support for transformative agreements after 2024. Continues to push for immediate open access to publicly funded research.

Plan S Diamond OA initiative

Academic Publishing Industry Trends (2024)

Market Growth: Open Access journal publishing revenues increased from $1.9B (2023) to $2.1B (2024), projected to reach $3.2B by 2028.

Continued Extraction: Despite two decades since the Berlin Declaration on Open Access, toll-access articles still represent the majority of scholarly output, with commercial publishers maintaining ~40% profit margins.
