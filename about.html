About the Movement

The Revolution Won't Be Paywalled

The Revolution Won't Be Paywalled (TRWBP) is a grassroots movement to reclaim scholarly publishing from extractive capitalism and build a truly open, scholar-governed scholarly commons.

We believe that:

  • Research funded by the public belongs to the public
  • Scholarly communication is infrastructure, not a profit center
  • Knowledge should be a commons, not a commodity
  • Scholars should govern the systems that disseminate their work
  • 37% profit margins on unpaid labour are unacceptable

Why "The Revolution Won't Be Paywalled"?

The name draws inspiration from Gil Scott-Heron's revolutionary protest poetry tradition, applying that energy to scholarly publishing. Just as "The Revolution Will Not Be Televised" challenged corporate control of information, we challenge corporate control of knowledge.

"The knowledge is ours. The expertise is ours. The legitimacy is ours. We've been giving them away. We can stop."

Our Vision: #ScholarlyCommons

We're building a movement around the concept of Scholarly Commonsâ€”a vision where scholarly communication operates as public infrastructure rather than private profit.

This means:

  • Diamond Open Access as the default (free to read, free to publish)
  • Scholar-governed platforms that serve researchers, not shareholders
  • Redirected funding from subscriptions to open infrastructure
  • Reformed assessment that values impact over journal brands
  • Global equity in access and participation

Why This Campaign Matters

The peer review crisis is real, but it's not primarily about processâ€”it's about economics. We can tinker with review models all we want, but until we address the fundamental extraction of wealth from the scholarly ecosystem, we're just rearranging deck chairs.

The numbers are clear:

  • Elsevier: Â£2.3 billion profit (2023), 37% margin
  • Higher than Apple (23%), Google (26%), Microsoft (34%)
  • Built on unpaid peer review, unpaid editing, publicly funded research
  • The Big Five control 50% of all research output

The alternatives work:

  • Journal of Machine Learning Research: Â£6.50 per article
  • Quantum: Â£489 per article, no profit
  • These are high-quality, well-regarded journals proving Diamond OA is viable

The solution is collective action:

  • Editorial board resignations (NeuroImage, Journal of Informetrics)
  • Institutional negotiations (UC System walking away from Elsevier)
  • Funder mandates (Plan S, UK Concordat)
  • Scholar-led infrastructure (Open Library of Humanities, JMLR, etc.)

Campaign Origins

This campaign emerged from years of frustration watching brilliant scholars donate their labour to enrich publishers while early career researchers struggle to afford APCs and Global South researchers can't access knowledge.

It crystallized around several key insights:

  1. The crisis isn't technicalâ€”it's economic
  2. We already know how to solve it (Diamond OA works)
  3. The money exists (just misdirected to profits)
  4. We have the power (prestige comes from scholars, not publishers)
  5. Collective action works (editorial board resignations, institutional walk-aways)
