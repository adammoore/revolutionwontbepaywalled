Subject: Proposal to Cancel/Renegotiate [PUBLISHER] Big Deal To: [PROVOST / LIBRARY DIRECTOR / RELEVANT ADMINISTRATOR] **Executive Summary:** I propose that [INSTITUTION] terminate (or substantially renegotiate) our subscription agreement with [PUBLISHER] at the next renewal point. Cost analysis demonstrates that we can improve access to research while reducing costs by approximately [¬£ AMOUNT OR %] annually. **Current Situation:** - Annual cost: ¬£[AMOUNT] - Cost per download: ¬£[AMOUNT] - Annual price increase: [%] - Articles by [INSTITUTION] authors published in [PUBLISHER] journals: [NUMBER] **The Problem:** 1. **Unsustainable costs**: Over the past [X] years, our costs have increased by [%] while our budget has [remained flat/increased by only %]. 2. **Poor value**: We're paying ¬£[AMOUNT] per article downloaded. Diamond OA alternatives cost ¬£300-500 per article published. 3. **Double-dipping**: We pay [PUBLISHER] ¬£[APC AMOUNT] to publish + ¬£[SUBSCRIPTION AMOUNT] to read back our own research. 4. **Inequitable access**: We're subsidizing a system that excludes researchers at less wealthy institutions. **Proposed Action:** **Option 1 - Aggressive Renegotiation:** - Demand [%] price reduction - Require a la carte cancellation rights for individual titles - Cap annual increases at [%] - Walk away if terms are not met **Option 2 - Full Cancellation:** - Cancel the Big Deal effective [DATE] - Redirect ¬£[AMOUNT] to: - Interlibrary loan budget: ¬£[AMOUNT] - Institutional repository infrastructure: ¬£[AMOUNT] - Diamond OA journal support: ¬£[AMOUNT] - Pay-per-view article access: ¬£[AMOUNT] - Net savings: ¬£[AMOUNT] **Supporting Evidence:** Similar institutions have successfully cancelled and maintained (or improved) access: - **University of California** (2019): Walked away from Elsevier, maintained research productivity - **[PEER INSTITUTION 1]**: Cancelled [YEAR], reported [%] decrease in access issues - **[PEER INSTITUTION 2]**: Renegotiated and saved ¬£[AMOUNT] annually **Access Mitigation:** Post-cancellation access strategies: 1. Interlibrary loan (48-72 hour turnaround) 2. Pay-per-view for urgent needs 3. Author preprints (70%+ of articles have free versions) 4. Direct author requests (87% response rate in studies) 5. Enhanced discovery tools for open access alternatives **Faculty Consultation:** I recommend forming a faculty advisory group to: - Review usage data by department - Identify essential vs. low-use journals - Explore discipline-specific OA alternatives - Build support for the transition **Financial Impact:** | Scenario | Year 1 Cost | Year 5 Cost | 5-Year Savings | |----------|-------------|-------------|----------------| | Status quo | ¬£[AMOUNT] | ¬£[AMOUNT] | ¬£0 | | Renegotiation | ¬£[AMOUNT] | ¬£[AMOUNT] | ¬£[AMOUNT] | | Cancellation | ¬£[AMOUNT] | ¬£[AMOUNT] | ¬£[AMOUNT] | **Timeline:** - [DATE]: Present proposal to library committee - [DATE]: Consult with faculty advisory group - [DATE]: Notify [PUBLISHER] of non-renewal (or renegotiation demands) - [DATE]: Implement transition plan - [DATE]: Big Deal ends / New agreement begins **Recommendation:** I recommend **[Option 1 / Option 2]** based on [RATIONALE]. This approach balances our commitment to faculty research needs with our responsibility to manage costs sustainably and support equitable knowledge infrastructure. I am prepared to lead the transition effort and work closely with faculty to ensure minimal disruption. Respectfully submitted, [YOUR NAME] [YOUR TITLE] [DATE] --- **Appendices:** - Detailed usage statistics by department - Cost per download analysis - Comparison with peer institutions - Timeline for similar cancellations at other universities - FAQ for faculty
