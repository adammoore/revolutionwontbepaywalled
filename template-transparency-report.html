Why Transparency Matters

Most faculty have no idea how much their institution spends on subscriptions or how publisher business models work. Transparency:

  • Empowers faculty to make informed publishing decisions
  • Builds support for library negotiations and cancellations
  • Reveals the "double-dipping" problem (paying to publish AND to read)
  • Demonstrates the cost-effectiveness of Diamond OA alternatives
  • Creates momentum for institutional policy changes
