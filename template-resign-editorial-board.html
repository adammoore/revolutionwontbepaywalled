Email Template

Copy and customize the text below

Subject: Resignation from Editorial Board of [JOURNAL NAME] Dear [EDITOR-IN-CHIEF / PUBLISHER CONTACT], I am writing to formally resign from my position as [POSITION] on the editorial board of [JOURNAL NAME], effective [DATE]. This decision has not been made lightly. I have greatly valued the opportunity to contribute to scholarly discourse in [FIELD] and to work with colleagues whose research I deeply respect. However, I can no longer justify lending my expertise and reputation to a publication model that I believe undermines the scholarly mission we all share. My concerns are threefold: 1. **Financial exploitation**: [JOURNAL NAME] charges [SUBSCRIPTION PRICE/APC AMOUNT], yet editorial board members receive [PAYMENT AMOUNT OR "no compensation"]. This represents a fundamental misalignment between the value we create and the profits extracted by [PUBLISHER NAME]. 2. **Access barriers**: Research published in [JOURNAL NAME] remains behind paywalls that prevent access by researchers in the Global South, independent scholars, and the public whose taxes often funded the research. This contradicts the fundamental purpose of scholarly communication. 3. **Sustainable alternatives exist**: Diamond Open Access journals such as [EXAMPLE IN YOUR FIELD] demonstrate that rigorous peer review and quality publishing can be achieved at a fraction of the cost, without paywalls or APCs. I believe that our scholarly community has the collective power to create publishing infrastructure that serves researchers rather than shareholders. I encourage [JOURNAL NAME] to transition to a Diamond Open Access model. Should you decide to pursue such a transition, I would be happy to discuss how I might support that effort. I wish you and the editorial team all the best, and I remain committed to supporting publishing models that genuinely serve the scholarly community. Sincerely, [YOUR NAME] [YOUR TITLE] [YOUR INSTITUTION] --- Optional addition for coordinated resignations: P.S. I am one of [NUMBER] editorial board members resigning today in coordinated action. A public statement will be released on [DATE], and we are actively working to establish a Diamond Open Access alternative in this field.

Successful Examples

  • NeuroImage Editorial Board (2023) - Mass resignation led to creation of Diamond OA alternative
  • Journal of Informetrics Team - Left to create Quantitative Science Studies (MIT Press, Diamond OA)
  • Lingua Editorial Board (2015) - All editors resigned to create Glossa (Diamond OA)
