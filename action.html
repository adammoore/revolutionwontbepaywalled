Take Action

The revolution is practical, actionable, and starting now. Choose the actions that fit your role and capacity.

For Researchers & Academics

âœ“ Publish in Diamond Open Access Venues

Why: Every article in a Diamond OA journal undermines the extractive model.

How:

  • Search for Diamond OA journals in your field at DOAJ.org
  • Ask colleagues about quality OA venues
  • Check if your learned society has an OA option
  • Consider preprint + overlay journals

Risk level: Low (many Diamond OA journals are high-quality and well-regarded)

âœ“ Refuse Exploitative Review Requests

Why: Your unpaid labour props up 37% profit margins.

How:

  • Check the journal's APC (refuse if > Â£1,000)
  • Check the subscription price (refuse if > Â£2,000)
  • Politely decline: "I prioritize reviewing for non-profit journals"
  • Suggest they contact you if they convert to Diamond OA

Risk level: Low (there are always other reviewers; your time is valuable)

âœ“ Resign from Commercial Editorial Boards

Why: Your reputation legitimizes their extraction.

How:

  • Calculate what the journal makes vs. what you're paid
  • Coordinate with other editors if possible
  • Announce publicly: "I've resigned from [Journal] due to exploitative practices"
  • Launch or join a Diamond OA alternative

Risk level: Medium (but high impact; many successful examples)

Examples: NeuroImage editorial board (2023), Journal of Informetrics team

âœ“ Always Deposit Preprints

Why: Makes your work freely accessible regardless of journal choice.

How:

  • Use arXiv, bioRxiv, SocArXiv, etc.
  • Deposit BEFORE journal submission
  • Update with final accepted version if allowed
  • Link to preprint in your email signature

Risk level: Very low (most journals allow preprints)

For Librarians

âœ“ Calculate the True Cost of Big Deals

Why: Transparency enables better negotiations.

How:

  • Calculate cost per article downloaded
  • Compare to Diamond OA alternatives
  • Share findings with faculty and administration
  • Publicize costs (if contracts allow)

âœ“ Negotiate Aggressively (or Walk Away)

Why: Publishers have more to lose than you do.

How:

  • Join consortial negotiations (RLUK, SCONUL, etc.)
  • Demand price freezes or reductions
  • Request rights to cancel individual titles
  • Be willing to walk away (see: UC System, Germany)

Examples: University of California walked away from Elsevier and thrived

âœ“ Redirect Funds to Open Infrastructure

Why: The money exists; it's just misdirected.

How:

  • Calculate savings from cancelled subscriptions
  • Invest in institutional repositories
  • Support Diamond OA initiatives
  • Fund scholar-led publishing platforms
  • Join Invest in Open Infrastructure
